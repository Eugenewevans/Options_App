What are Options? 🤔

Options are financial contracts that give you the right (but not the obligation) to buy or sell a stock at a specific price within a certain time period.

Strike Price 🎯

The strike price is the predetermined price at which you can buy or sell the stock when you exercise your option.

Call Example: Apple stock is $150, you buy a call with $145 strike. You can buy Apple for $145 even if it goes to $170!
Put Example: Tesla stock is $800, you buy a put with $810 strike. You can sell Tesla for $810 even if it drops to $700!

Call Options 📈

Call options give you the right to BUY a stock at the strike price. You buy calls when you think the stock price will go UP. If the stock rises above the strike price, your call option becomes more valuable!

Put Options 📉

Put options give you the right to SELL a stock at the strike price. You buy puts when you think the stock price will go DOWN. If the stock falls below the strike price, your put option becomes more valuable!

Money Status

In-the-Money (ITM): Call option when stock price > strike price, Put option when stock price < strike price
At-the-Money (ATM): When stock price = strike price
Out-of-the-Money (OTM): Call option when stock price < strike price, Put option when stock price > strike price

Bullish Strategy

When you expect a stock to go UP:

  • Buy CALL options
  • Look for positive news about the company
  • Watch for earnings reports, product launches
  • Monitor sector-wide positive trends

Bearish Strategy

When you expect a stock to go DOWN:

  • Buy PUT options
  • Look for negative news about the company
  • Watch for poor earnings, regulatory issues
  • Monitor sector-wide negative trends

Risk Management

  • Don't invest all your money in one stock
  • Diversify across different sectors
  • Pay attention to market trends
  • Use the market trends panel to guide decisions

Essential Options Terms

Premium: The price you pay for an option ($200 in this game)
Strike Price: The predetermined price at which you can buy/sell the stock when exercising your option
In-the-Money (ITM): When an option has intrinsic value (call: stock price > strike, put: stock price < strike)
At-the-Money (ATM): When the stock price equals the strike price
Out-of-the-Money (OTM): When an option has no intrinsic value (call: stock price < strike, put: stock price > strike)
Expiration: When the option expires (next day in this game)
Bullish: Expecting the market or stock to go UP
Bearish: Expecting the market or stock to go DOWN
Volatility: How much a stock price moves up and down

The Greeks

Delta (D): How much the option price changes when the stock price moves $1
Gamma (G): How much Delta changes when the stock price moves $1
Theta (T): How much the option loses value each day (time decay)
Vega (V): How much the option price changes when volatility changes

Understanding the Greeks

Delta (D): Measures how much your option price changes when the stock moves $1. Higher Delta = more sensitive to stock price changes.
Gamma (G): Shows how quickly Delta changes. High Gamma = option value changes rapidly with stock movement.
Theta (T): Time decay - how much value your option loses each day. Always negative for buyers.
Vega (V): Sensitivity to volatility changes. Higher Vega = more affected by market uncertainty.

Greek Strategy Tips

High Delta Options: More expensive but more responsive to stock price changes
High Gamma Options: Great for big moves but lose value quickly if wrong
High Theta Options: Lose value faster over time - better for short-term trades
High Vega Options: Good when you expect volatility to increase

Game Application

Short Expiration: Higher Theta (time decay) - use for quick trades
Long Expiration: Lower Theta - better for holding longer
Volatile Stocks: Higher Vega - good for news-driven trades
Stable Stocks: Lower Vega - more predictable pricing

Strike Prices: ITM options are more expensive but have higher probability of profit. OTM options are cheaper but riskier!
